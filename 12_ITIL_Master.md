# ITIL Master Certification Guide (2025-2026)

## Overview
ITIL (Information Technology Infrastructure Library) Master is the highest level of ITIL certification path, validating expertise in IT Service Management principles and practices across the complete service lifecycle.

**Certification Path:** Foundation → Intermediate (Service Management & Lifecycle) → Expert → Master

---

## ITIL Certification Pathway

### Level 1: Foundation (Beginner)
- **Exam:** ITIL Foundation v4
- **Duration:** 2-3 months study
- **Fee:** $150-200 USD
- **Focus:** ITSM basics and terminology

### Level 2: Intermediate (Practitioner)
- **Pathway A - Service Management:**
  - Plan, Implement, Manage (PIM)
  - Create, Deliver (CD)
  - Drive Stakeholder (DSV)
- **Pathway B - Lifecycle:**
  - Direct, Plan, Implement (DPI)
  - Design & Transition (D&T)
  - Operate, Support (OS)
  - Continual Service (CS)
- **Each Exam:** 3-4 months study, $200-250 USD

### Level 3: Expert (Advanced)
- **Exam:** ITIL Expert Transition exam
- **Duration:** 6-12 months study
- **Fee:** $250-300 USD
- **Prerequisite:** Foundation + at least 1 intermediate module

### Level 4: Master (Highest Level)
- **Assessment:** Portfolio-based assessment
- **Duration:** 12-24 months of applied practice
- **Fee:** $500-1000 USD
- **Requirement:** Complete Foundation + Intermediate (4 modules) + Expert

## Master Level Assessment

### Portfolio-Based Assessment
- **Duration:** 24 months minimum
- **Content:** Demonstrate mastery through:
  1. Professional Summary (10 pages)
  2. Four Applied Experience Reports (15 pages each)
  3. Two Service Management Scenarios (10 pages each)
  4. Comprehensive Portfolio (50+ pages)

### Assessment Criteria
- **Depth:** Understanding of all ITIL domains
- **Breadth:** Application across different contexts
- **Real-World Application:** Proven implementation experience
- **Integration:** Connection between modules
- **Value Delivery:** Demonstrated service outcomes

### Topics for Master Assessment
1. **Service Value System**
2. **Four Dimensions of ITSM**
3. **Service Lifecycle Integration**
4. **Guiding Principles**
5. **ITSM Practices Integration**
6. **Organizational Context**
7. **Change & Innovation**
8. **Measurement & Improvement**

## ITIL v4 Key Changes (2019)

### From v3 to v4
- **Service Value System (SVS)** replacing lifecycle
- **Guiding Principles** (7 key principles)
- **Practices** (34 practices)
- **Four Dimensions:**
  - Organizations and people
  - Information and technology
  - Partners and suppliers
  - Value streams and processes

### 7 Guiding Principles
1. Focus on value
2. Start where you are
3. Progress iteratively
4. Collaborate and promote visibility
5. Think and work holistically
6. Keep it simple and practical
7. Optimize and automate

## Exam Tips

### Foundation Tips
- **Time:** ~1 minute per question
- **Terminology:** Know ITIL definitions
- **Focus:** Understand processes, not memorize

### Intermediate Tips
- **Application:** Real-world scenarios
- **Integration:** Connection between modules
- **Best Practices:** Industry standard approaches
- **Time Management:** Pace yourself

### Master Assessment Tips
- **Evidence-Based:** Show real experience
- **Integration:** Connect all domains
- **Reflection:** Demonstrate learning
- **Innovation:** Show continuous improvement
- **Documentation:** Professional presentation
