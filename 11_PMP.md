# PMP (Project Management Professional) Guide (2025-2026)

## Overview
PMP is the highest-level project management certification validating expertise in initiating, planning, executing, monitoring, and closing projects according to PMBOK (Project Management Body of Knowledge).

**Certification Code:** PMP (PMBOK 7th Edition)

---

## Duration & Study Timeline
- **Recommended Study Duration:** 4-6 months
- **Study Hours Required:** 250-400 hours
- **Experience Requirement:** 4,500-7,500 hours project management

---

## Exam Details

### Exam Duration
- **Total Duration:** 230 minutes (3 hours 50 minutes)
- **Total Questions:** 180 questions
- **Passing Score:** 106/180 correct (59%)
- **Question Format:** Multiple choice with real-world scenarios

### Exam Fee (2025-2026)
- **PMI Members:** $405 USD
- **Non-Members:** $555 USD
- **PMI Membership:** $139/year
- **Total First Year (Non-member):** $694 USD
- **Retake Fee:** $405-555 USD

### Prerequisites
- **Bachelor's Degree Required:**
  - 4,500 hours project management experience (3 years), OR
  - 7,500 hours without degree (5 years)
- **Education/Training:** 35 contact hours minimum

## Knowledge Areas (Traditional PMBOK)

### 10 Knowledge Areas:

1. **Scope Management**
   - Define, verify, and control scope

2. **Schedule Management**
   - Plan, define, sequence, estimate, develop, and control schedule

3. **Cost Management**
   - Plan, estimate, budget, and control costs

4. **Quality Management**
   - Plan, manage, and control quality

5. **Resource Management**
   - Plan, acquire, develop, and manage resources

6. **Communications Management**
   - Plan, manage, and monitor communications

7. **Risk Management**
   - Plan, identify, analyze, respond, and monitor risks

8. **Procurement Management**
   - Plan, conduct, control, and close procurements

9. **Stakeholder Management**
   - Identify, plan, manage, and monitor stakeholders

10. **Integration Management**
    - Plan, direct, manage, and control work

## Exam Tips

1. **Read Carefully:** Questions are detailed and specific
2. **Context is Key:** Understand project environment
3. **Best Practices:** Answer based on PMBOK recommendations
4. **Risk First:** Risk management emphasis in newer versions
5. **People Focus:** Heavy emphasis on leadership and soft skills

## Common Mistakes

1. ❌ Insufficient experience documentation
2. ❌ Weak time and schedule management knowledge
3. ❌ Underestimating people management importance
