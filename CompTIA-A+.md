# CompTIA A+ Certification Guide

The CompTIA A+ certification is an entry-level certification for IT professionals, focusing on fundamental knowledge of computer hardware, software, and basic troubleshooting. It is widely recognized in the industry as a standard for candidates seeking to start a career in IT.

## Overview
- **Certification Name**: CompTIA A+
- **Exam Codes**: 220-1101, 220-1102
- **Number of Questions**: 90
- **Test Duration**: 90 minutes
- **Passing Score**: 675 (on a scale of 100-900)

## Exam Objectives
1. **Mobile Devices**: Installation and configuration of laptops and other mobile devices.
2. **Networking**: Understanding networking concepts and configurations, such as TCP/IP, wireless networking, and diagnosing connectivity issues.
3. **Hardware**: Identification, installation, and troubleshooting of computer components.
4. **Virtualization and Cloud Computing**: Understanding the benefits and types of virtualization, and cloud computing concepts.
5. **Security**: Basic security concepts, identifying security threats, and applying best practices to secure devices and networks.
6. **Software Troubleshooting**: Identifying and resolving software issues on various operating systems, including Windows, macOS, Linux, and mobile OS.
7. **Operational Procedures**: Understanding best practices in support and troubleshooting methodologies, as well as professionalism and communication.
